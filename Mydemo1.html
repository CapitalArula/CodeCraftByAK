Cardiology

A branch of medicine that deals with disorders of the heart and the cardiovascular system.

Cardiology is a medical specialty and a branch of internal medicine concerned with disorders of the heart. It deals with the diagnosis and treatment of such conditions as congenital heart defects, coronary artery disease, electrophysiology, heart failure and valvular heart disease. Subspecialties of the cardiology field include cardiac electrophysiology, echocardiography, interventional cardiology and nuclear cardiology.

Neurology

An expert in the diagnosis and treatment of diseases of the brain and nervous system.

It is a subspeciality. Neurologists have to do a good deal of internal medicine as many other diseases have neurological manifestations.

Hepatology

A branch of medicine concerned with the study, prevention, diagnosis, and management of diseases that affect the liver, gallbladder, biliary tree, and pancreas.

A hepatologist's focus is limited to only the liver and the bile ducts.

Pediatrics

The branch of medicine dealing with the health and medical care of infants, children, and adolescents from birth up to the age of 18.

Manage medical conditions affecting infants, children and young people. You'll see a wide range of patients, from babies through to teenagers.

Pediatricians study pediatrics, both of which stem from a form of the Greek pais "children." The remainder of the word comes from the Greek iatros "healer."

Frequently Asked Questions

Doctor job interview questions and answers

Questions about medical experience and background
1. What do you like most about practicing medicine and why?
2. What aspects of practicing medicine do find most challenging? ...
3. What is your philosophy with medicine and treating patients?
4. How do you collaborate with other physicians and nurses?

Here are four questions you can ask at your next appointment start the conversation.

* Do I really need this test, treatment or procedure?
* What are the downsides?
* Are there simpler, safer options?
* What happens if I do nothing?

Please describe your Functional Medicine training. Do you take insurance? Or are you a cash practice? What are the main therapies that you use? (examples: dietary, chiropractic adjustments, supplements, prescription medications) Are they typically covered by insurance?

Asking about health/life (Elementary)

1. How are you?
2. How are things?
3. How's things?
4. How's it going?
5. How are you getting on?
6. How have you been?
7. What have you been (getting) up to?
8. I hope everything's okay?

Here are a few examples:

'Hey, how have you been lately? What's been happening? '
'You haven't seemed yourself lately. Is there something you'd like to talk about? '
